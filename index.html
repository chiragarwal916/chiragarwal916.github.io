Autonomous Vehicles: Munich - Projects

o In a joint intensive course by Cal-Poly and HM Munich, our team was tasked with developing the control system and simulating test cases for autonomous vehicle operation in hardware-in-the-loop, simulating our results in IPG Carmaker. A sample of test cases and the results are shown in the top left and bottom right.

o In the top right, our team had to simulate an avoidance maneuver of a vehicle, using dynamic equations to calculate various states, minimizing tire forces and slip angles to make the turns more efficient. To simplify this model, assumptions included a coasting vehicle, a small slip angle of max 6 degrees, and that the tire force must also not exceed a fourth of the vehicle mass. Our team recommended minimizing the mass and decreasing the distance from the COG to the rear wheels.

o In the bottom left, our goal was to maintain the vehicle positions between lanes by optimizing PID gains while minimizing tire forces and slip angles. Our team investigated the application of each type of gain on the system, made assumptions about realistic conditions, and applied corrections. Our team received an A-equivalency.

Senior Design Project, Aerotech Inc.

o This project aimed to find the potential source of inconsistencies in low payload motors, with a 4 person team.

o A magnetic field tester was created to test the hypothesis that these inconsistencies may stem from non-uniformity in the fields of these magnetic shafts.

o The remotely-developed machine saved Aerotech over two thousand dollars a week by preventing reassembly of motors and wasted material used in assembly.

o The autonomous machine can measure voltage fluctuations of about 34mV with a 95% confidence precision, and graphically analyzes and compares magnetic field against previous scans to determine compatibility

o We achieved significant unit cost savings by negotiating application of custom vs OEM components in design, allowing for remote prototype development during Covid-19. We received an A+.
